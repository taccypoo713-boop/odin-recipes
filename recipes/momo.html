MoMo

Recipe

Momo is a popular Nepali dumpling made of thin wheat flour wrappers filled with seasoned minced meat (usually chicken, buffalo, or pork) or vegetables, then steamed or sometimes fried. It’s served hot with a spicy, tangy tomato-based achar (dipping sauce). Soft, juicy on the inside, and comforting, momo is one of the most loved street foods and comfort foods in Nepal.

Ingredients

  • Flour
  • salt
  • water
  • coriander
  • minced meat or vegetables
  • onion
  • masala
  • msg
  • chilli
  • garlic
  • ginger
  • mustard oil
  • tomato
  • dried red chilli
  • timur
  • white sesame seeds

Steps

  1. Make the dough: Mix all-purpose flour, water, and a pinch of salt. Knead until smooth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Prepare the filling: Mix minced meat or vegetables with chopped onion, garlic, ginger, green chili, coriander, salt, pepper, and soy sauce.
  3. Shape the momos: Roll dough into small circles, place a spoonful of filling in the center, and fold/seal the edges.
  4. Steam the momos: Place them in a steamer and cook for 10–12 minutes until done.
  5. Make the dip (achar): Grind tomatoes, red chili, garlic, ginger, sesame, salt, and lemon juice.
  6. Serve: Enjoy hot momos with the spicy achar.
